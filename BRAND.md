# sunday — Brand Guidelines

> **For Claude Code:** Load this file at the start of every session involving sunday. Apply these guidelines to all generated copy, UI, emails, ads, and landing pages without being asked. The brand name is always written as **sunday** — never Sunday.

---

## 1. Brand Snapshot

sunday is a restaurant payment and operations platform that removes friction from checkout — for guests, servers, and operators. It saves 12 minutes per table, drives 5× more Google reviews, and lifts average tips from 20% to 30%. The mission: make the world's fastest and easiest way to pay at restaurants. sunday doesn't pile on more tech — it amplifies what already works.

**Core audience:** Multi-unit restaurant operators (QSR to Michelin-starred), restaurant staff, and diners.

**Mission statement:** Make the world's fastest and easiest way to pay at restaurants.

**Positioning:** We don't pile on more tech. We amplify what already works.

---

## 2. Brand Values

sunday is built on three core values. Every piece of copy should reflect all three.

### Simple
Clarity above everything. Be as straight to the point as possible. No fillers, no fluffy language, no complicated sentences. Readers must immediately understand what you're explaining, how it works, and how they'll benefit.

### Trust
Give clear, honest, practical advice. Always explain the "how" and "why" — never just state a claim. No vague explanations, no "bs." sunday guides restaurant owners through digitizing their business and must be a reliable expert at every step.

### Beyond
sunday is friendly, open, quirky, confident, straightforward, and hard to forget. The brand stands out — and how it communicates should be the same. sunday's personality should show, but never overshadow the mission.

---

## 3. Brand Personality

If sunday were a person, friends would describe them as: **friendly, open, quirky, confident, straightforward, and hard to forget.**

sunday is NOT basic, distant, or elitist. It writes in a warm and relatable way. It's a young company with young energy — and that must show.

### What sunday IS

| Trait | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Ethical** | Inclusive, unbiased, and non-discriminatory. Words are powerful — use them carefully. |
| **Expert & guide** | Knowledgeable but never condescending. Always explain the how and why. Encourage action. |
| **Dynamic** | Cool, informal, practical. Talks directly to the reader. Asks questions to engage. |
| **Authentic** | Subtle, objective. Shares imperfections instead of hiding them. Human beings are behind sunday — the audience should feel it. |

### What sunday is NOT

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| **Passive & impersonal** | Sounds boring, old-fashioned, distant. sunday is warm and relatable, never robotic. |
| **Erratic** | Adjusting tone too wildly. Always keep it professional and coherent, even when playful. |
| **Excessive or pretentious** | Be direct, honest, and emphatic — not over-the-top familiar or showy. |

## 4. Voice & Tone

The voice is constant. The tone adjusts by context.

| Touchpoint | Tone | Key rule |
|---|---|---|
| Ads | Confident, punchy, human | Hook in 3 words. No jargon. Relatable truth first. |
| Landing pages | Clear, guiding, outcome-led | Clear > clever. Data backs the claim. |
| Emails | Warm, conversational, specific | Subject line = the point. One ask per email. |
| Product / UI | Relaxed but professional | 8 words or fewer for UI copy. Never blame the user. |
| Social media | Playful, real, energetic | Maximum personality. Short. Shareable. Ask questions. |
| Error messages | Calm, clear, supportive | Tell what happened and what to do. Never "oops." |

## 5. Writing Rules

### Active voice
Always prefer active voice. Don't be vague.
- ✅ "Click here to pay"
- ❌ "Proceeding a payment needs you to click on the button"

### Ask questions
Questions are a simple, effective way to engage. Use them when relevant — but don't overdo it.
- ✅ "How many times have you had to wait 10+ minutes for the bill?"
- ❌ "Waiting for the bill can take a while!"

### Be specific
Start with a relatable truth the reader has experienced.
- ✅ "Have you waited 15 minutes for your bill?"
- ❌ "Tough dinner experience?"

### Direct address
Talk directly to the reader. Use "you" and "your restaurant," not "users" or "clients."

### Use lists
Break things into bulleted or numbered lists. Never write condensed, long paragraphs.

### Numbers
- Spell out numbers that begin a sentence: "One client just paid with sunday."
- Use numerals mid-sentence: "There's almost 200 people in this restaurant."

### Emojis
Emojis are welcome in newsletters, social posts, and emails — they add fun. Use with caution and moderation. Avoid predictable emojis (🚀 🦄). Place after a word, not mid-sentence.
- ✅ "Hey, it's sunday 👋, how are you?"
- ❌ "👋, It's sunday 🆒, how are you? 😊"

### Acronyms & abbreviations
If it's not common knowledge, spell it out the first time, then use the short version.
- ✅ First time: "Point of sale (POS)" / after: "POS"
- ❌ First time: "POS" (without explanation)

### Plain language
Speak in the restaurant owner's language. Use the words they use. Avoid unnecessary jargon. If a technical term is unavoidable, explain it with an example.

### Coherence
Be consistent throughout a single piece of copy. Don't mix phrasing styles, instruction formats, or addressing conventions within one asset.

## 6. Grammar & Mechanics

### Brand name
**sunday is always lowercase.** No exceptions.
- ✅ sunday
- ❌ Sunday

### Capitalization
- **Capitalize:** WiFi, QR codes, POS, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Visa, Instagram, Facebook, and any other acronym or company name.
- **Do not capitalize:** internet, web, hashtag.
- **Sentences:** Always capitalize the first letter of a sentence.

### Punctuation
Use normal, standard punctuation. Follow grammar rules of the language being used.

### Product copy length
UI indications of 8 words or fewer are 100% understood. Aim for brevity. When context requires more, prioritize clarity and usefulness over hitting a word count.

### No "oops" or "whoops"
Avoid childish error language. Users are adults — treat them that way.
- ✅ "Sorry. Unless you've got a time machine, that content isn't available."
- ❌ "Woopsi daisy! Looks like something went completely wrong!"

### Don't blame the user
Error messages should focus on what to do next, not what the user did wrong. Avoid "you did" or "you didn't."
- ✅ "Enter in the format: name@example.com"
- ❌ "Please do not enter special characters in the password field."

## 7. Copy Formulas by Touchpoint

### Ads

**Headline formula:**
```
[Relatable restaurant moment or pain point] → [sunday outcome]
```

Examples:
- "Still waiting for the bill? sunday ends the wait."
- "More Google reviews. Less awkward asking."
- "Tips are up 30%. Staff noticed."

**Rules:**
- 1–2 sentences max for body copy.
- Lead with a human truth, close with a metric or outcome.
- One CTA only.

**Approved CTAs:** "See how it works" / "Get a free demo" / "Try it free" / "Discover sunday"

**Banned CTAs:** "Click here" / "Submit" / "Learn more" (too generic) / "Buy now" (wrong register)

**Character limits:**
- Instagram caption (above fold): ≤125 chars
- LinkedIn headline: ≤150 chars
- Display ad headline: ≤30 chars
- Display ad description: ≤90 chars

---

### Landing Pages

**Above-the-fold formula:**
```
[Outcome-led headline, ≤8 words]
[1-sentence sub: who it's for + the pain it solves]
[Primary CTA button] + [Soft CTA: "See how it works →"]
[Trust signal: logo strip, metric, or review count]
```

Example:
> **The checkout your guests actually love.**
> sunday helps restaurant operators close tables faster, earn more tips, and collect reviews automatically.
> [Get a free demo] [See how it works →]
> *Trusted by 10,000+ restaurants across 15 countries*

**Page flow:**
1. Hook — above the fold
2. Problem — acknowledge the pain in their words
3. Solution — show don't tell; use product UI
4. Proof — metrics, quotes, logos
5. CTA — one clear next step

**Headline style per section:**
- Problem: Empathetic, mirrors internal monologue ("Still waiting for your POS to catch up?")
- Solution: Outcome-first ("Check closed. Guest happy. Review in.")
- Proof: Data + voice ("'Our tips went up 30% in two weeks.' — Emma, GM")

---

### Emails

**Subject line rules:**
- Be straight to the point and informative from the subject line to the last word.
- Subject line and first sentence should both clearly announce what's new.
- No filler words, no vague teases.

| Type | Example (do) | Example (don't) |
|---|---|---|
| Feature | "New tool for your servers!" | "There's a change underway in your restaurant!" |
| Feature (descriptive) | "Now, your servers can manage their tables with a smartphone!" | "That was your dream and sunday did it." |

**Intro sentence:**
Lead with the benefit immediately.
- ✅ "In order to make your servers' lives easier, our new feature allows them to manage their tables from their smartphone."
- ❌ "That was your dream and sunday did it. The simplest way to pay in restaurants!"

**Body rules:**
- Short paragraphs: 2–3 sentences max.
- One ask per email. Two asks = two emails.
- Bullets for lists of 3+ items.
- CTA: action verb + object ("Book my demo" not "Submit")

**Sign-off:** Warm, real. Use first name or "The sunday team."
- ✅ "Cheers," / "Talk soon," / "See you there,"
- ❌ "Best regards," / "Sincerely," / "Warm regards,"

---

### Product & UI Copy

- Relaxed but professional. Product is more serious than social media.
- Crystal clear, not technical. Write for humans.
- Keep user actions specific and logical.
- Less is more: aim for 8 words or fewer in UI indications.
- Don't blame the user in error states.

Error message examples:
- ✅ "Your payment has been cancelled as the time frame has elapsed. Please try again."
- ❌ "Damn, you've taken too much time to pay. Next time it can work!"

## 8. Inclusive & Ethical Language

sunday is an equal opportunity brand. Content must always be accessible and free of bias.

- **Gender-neutral terms:** Use "server" not "waiter/waitress" (US). Use "diners" or "guests" not "clients" or "customers" (US; UK may vary).
- **Avoid stereotypes:** Never associate positions of power with any specific gender. Don't link features to a gender or sexual orientation.
- **Avoid identity references** unless objectively relevant to the content (race, religion, age, disability, orientation, ethnicity).
- **Careful word choices:** Avoid "crazy," "insane," or "mad" — these stigmatize people with mental disabilities.
- **Don't be condescending:** Never talk down. Help, guide, and encourage.

## 9. Localization

### US English
- Use "diners" or "guests" — not "clients" or "customers"
- Use "server(s)" — not "waiter" or "waitress"
- Date format: MM/DD/YY, HR:MIN AM/PM
- Currency: $XXX,XXX,XXX.YY (e.g., $100,000,000.98)

### UK English
- "Customers" may be used; "waiter" is still gender-neutral
- Date format: DD/MM/YY, MIN:HR AM/PM
- Currency: £XXX,XXX,XXX.YY (e.g., £100,000,000.98)

### French
- Always use "vouvoiement" (vous, not tu)
- System actions (triggered by sunday after a click): use infinitif — "Payer l'addition" ✅ not "Payez votre addition" ❌
- User actions (guest must do something): use impératif — "Saisissez votre nom" ✅ not "Saisir mon nom" ❌
- Currency: XXX XXX XXX,YY€ (e.g., 100 000 000,98€)

- **The wordmark** — "sunday" in lowercase Helvetica Neue Regular. Always lowercase. No exceptions.

| `Sunday Logo Horizontal Primary Transparent@2x.png` | Magenta mark + black wordmark, transparent BG | Use on light or white custom backgrounds |

```
Background?
├── White or light neutral  →  Logo Horizontal Primary (or Primary Transparent)
├── Magenta (#FF17E9)       →  Secondary Pink
├── Black                   →  Secondary Black
└── Dark photo / dark color →  NB Black Transparent
```

**Logo rules**
- Minimum digital size: 80px wide (horizontal lockup); 32px (mark only)
- Clear space: equal to the height of the "s" on all sides
- Never stretch, distort, or rotate the mark or wordmark
- Never recolor outside the approved palette (magenta mark; black or white wordmark)
- Never add drop shadows, gradients, outlines, or effects
- Never place on a busy photo without a solid color backdrop or overlay
- The mark may be used alone only at very small sizes (favicons, app icons) — always pair with the wordmark otherwise

**Primary brand color:** Hot magenta `#FF17E9` — sunday's signature. Commands attention. Use for one dominant element per layout.

**Color rules:**
- `#FF17E9` magenta is the hero color — it commands attention. Use it for one dominant element per layout, not everywhere.
- Secondary colors are for data visualization, tags, status indicators, and supporting sections. Never let them compete with magenta.
- All text on colored backgrounds must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum).
- Black text on white or light grey is the default readable state.

**Typefaces:**

sunday uses exactly 2 typefaces. Together they create harmony between precision and personality.

| Role | Font | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | **Helvetica Neue** | All UI, body copy, headlines, CTAs, and product text. Font file: `Sunday_Brant.otf` |
| Accent | **Handwritten type** | Used for expressive brand moments, callouts, and campaign visuals — not for UI or body copy |

**Helvetica Neue rules (apply everywhere):**

- **Weight:** Regular only. No bold, no italic, no underline, no other styles — ever.
- **Case:** Always lowercase for the sunday wordmark. Sentence case for all other copy.
- **Letter-spacing (tracking):**
  - Headlines & titles: **−5%**
  - Paragraphs: **−3%**
- **Line-spacing (leading):**
  - Headlines & titles: **90%**
  - Paragraphs & small type: **110%**

**Type scale (web):**

| Level | Desktop | Mobile |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | 56–72px | 36–44px |
| H2 | 40–48px | 28–32px |
| H3 | 28–32px | 22–24px |
| Body | 16–18px | 15–16px |
| Caption | 13–14px | 13px |

**Typography rules:**
- Sentence case everywhere. No title case. No ALL CAPS except for short label chips (max 2 words).
- Helvetica Neue Regular is the only permitted style. No bold, italic, or underline in brand copy (hyperlinks excepted).
- Handwritten type is for brand expression only — never for functional copy (buttons, forms, error messages, body text).

## 11. What to Always Avoid

**Banned words & phrases:**
- `leverage`, `synergy`, `seamless`, `robust`, `scalable solution`, `omnichannel`, `cutting-edge`, `best-in-class`, `paradigm shift`, `innovative`
- "We are pleased to inform you…" / "Please do not hesitate to…" / "Best regards,"
- Vague teases in email subject lines ("There's a change underway!")
- "Oops," "whoops," "woopsi daisy" in error messages

**Banned structural patterns:**
- Passive voice as default
- Long, condensed paragraphs (use lists instead)
- Starting an email or ad opener with "We" as the first word
- Three or more sentences of identical length in a row (vary the rhythm)
- Giving orders or being abrupt ("Do this. Now do this.")
- Being excessive, overly familiar, or pretentious

**Banned visual patterns:**
- Drop shadows or gradients on the logo
- More than two fonts in a single layout
- Capital "S" in sunday — ever
